BJAK Motor Insurance - Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What documents are needed for motor insurance claim?
A: Required documents: 1) Claim form, 2) Copy of policy, 3) FIR copy (if applicable), 4) Repair estimate from authorized garage, 5) Photos of damaged vehicle, 6) Driving license copy, 7) Registration certificate copy.

Q: How do I file a motor insurance claim?
A: Follow these steps: 1) Inform BJAK within 24 hours, 2) File FIR if third-party involved, 3) Take vehicle to network garage, 4) Submit required documents, 5) Claim is verified and processed, 6) Payment released to garage or reimbursed.

Q: What is covered under motor insurance?
A: Comprehensive policy covers: 1) Own damage from accidents, natural calamities, fire, theft, 2) Third-party liability for property damage and bodily injury, 3) Personal accident cover for owner-driver.

Q: What is not covered in motor insurance?
A: Exclusions: 1) Normal wear and tear, 2) Driving under influence, 3) War and nuclear perils, 4) Consequential losses, 5) Depreciation and consumables, 6) Damage outside geographical area.

Q: How long does motor claim take to settle?
A: Cashless claims at network garages: 3-5 days. Reimbursement claims: 7-15 days. Third-party claims may take longer depending on court proceedings.

Q: What is IDV in motor insurance?
A: IDV (Insured Declared Value) is the current market value of your vehicle. It is calculated as: Ex-showroom price - Depreciation. IDV determines your premium and claim amount.

Q: Can I transfer my no-claim bonus?
A: Yes, NCB can be transferred to: 1) New vehicle purchased within 90 days, 2) Another policy in your name. NCB cannot be transferred to another person.

Q: What is third-party motor insurance?
A: Third-party insurance covers damages caused to third-party property or person. It is mandatory by law. It does not cover damage to your own vehicle.

Q: How do I add accessories to my motor policy?
A: Inform BJAK about accessories before policy renewal. Accessories value is added to IDV. Additional premium is charged based on accessory value. Keep bills as proof.

Q: What is the claim process for total loss?
A: For total loss: 1) Inform BJAK immediately, 2) Submit all required documents, 3) Vehicle inspection by surveyor, 4) If total loss confirmed, IDV is paid minus depreciation, 5) Vehicle ownership transferred to insurer.
